Pharmacy

Range of Medicines

Offers the most complete range of medicines available in our country. These include over-the-counter (OTC) and prescription (Rx) medicine.

Prescription (Rx) Medicines

Prescription (Rx) are medicines ordered by a doctor and are only available from a pharmacist.

Over the Counter (OTC) Medicines

Over the counter (OTC) are medicines which can be bought without a doctor's prescription.

Helping Hands Care Supplies

Helping Hands Care medical supplies, medicines, bandages, supplies for selfcare.

Basic Usage

Painkiller

Take 1 tablet every 4-6 hrs within 24hr period, first dose you may take 2 tablets in the 1st hr, don't exceed 2 tablets within 12hr period.

Antibiotic

Take 1 antibiotic tablet if needed, do not share, keep track of doses.

Anti-Allergic

Should be taken every day as needed, take about 1-3 hours to work, preferably to take it before bedtime.

Common Cold

Take medicines only as the label says, adults & children 12yrs older 2 caplets every 4 hrs, children under 12 ask a doctor.
